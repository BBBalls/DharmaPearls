The Related Discourses

13. The Factors of the Noble Path (cf. SN 45: Maggasaṃyutta)

This group of sutras has the common theme of describing the eight factors of the noble path, which in English is commonly called the eightfold path. These factors are right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These eight factors serve as a guide to the Buddhist practice, and the eightfold path is the definition of the fourth noble truth.

This group consists of 46 full sutras plus 68 abbreviated sutra variants. Below is a list of the English translations currently available with brief synopses of their contents. Pali equivalents are listed in () and the sourcetext is indicated in [].

Note: All titles for SA sutras are supplied by the translator as an aid for the reader.

1. The First Sign (SN 45.55: Accomplishment in Proper Attention (1st) ) [T 99.198b5]

Right view is the first sign of liberation like the first light of dawn is the first sign of sunrise.

2. Forerunners (SN 45.1: Ignorance) [T 99.198b14]

When producing insight is the forerunner, good things, conscience, right view, and the eightfold path lead to liberation.

3. The Roots of Good and Evil [T 99.198b26]

The root of all evil is ignorance, and the root of all good is insight.

4. The Right Task (SN 45.24: Practice (2nd)) [T 99.198c14]

Following the eightfold path is the correct task for lay and renunciant practitioners.

5. Desire (SN 45.30: With Uttiya) [T 99.198c27]

Desire is not the thing desired but the craving of it. The eightfold path is the way to ending this problem.

6. Deathlessness (SN 45.7: A Mendicant (2nd)) [T 99.199a13]

The Buddha explains that the deathless in his teaching is the end of the contaminants that lead to rebirth.

7. Requirements (SN 45.28: Immersion) [T 99.199a22]

The Buddha explains that the first seven parts of the eightfold path are the roots and requirements of right concentration.

8. Desire (2) (SN 45.30: With Uttiya) [T 99.199b03]

Desire is not the thing desired but the craving of it. The eightfold path is the way to ending this problem.

9. Deathlessness (2) (SN 45.7: A Mendicant (2nd)) [T 99.199b03]

The Buddha explains that the deathless in his teaching is the end of the contaminants that lead to rebirth.

10. Requirements (2) (SN 45.28: Immersion) [T 99.199b03]

The Buddha explains that the first seven parts of the eightfold path are the roots and requirements of right concentration.

11. Fear of Losing Mother and Son (AN 3.62: Perils ) [T 99.199b5]

The Buddha describes three common fears of losing one’s mother or son during disasters, but he says those fears really come from sickness, old age, and death. The way to escaping these fears is the eightfold path.

12. Sensation (SN 45.29: Feeling) [T 99.199c17]

The eightfold path is the way to end the three sensations.

13. Disagreeable Things (AN 10.76: Three Things) [T 99.199c27]

The eightfold path is the way to end old age, illness, and death.

14. Trainees and Non-Trainees (SN 45.13: A Trainee) [T 99.200a14]

The terms “trainee” and “non-trainee” refer to disciples who are learning or have finished learning the eightfold path.

15. Correct Men and Great Men [T 99.200a22]

The terms “correct man” and “great man” refer to disciples who are learning or have finished learning the eightfold path.

16. The Noble End of Contaminants [T 99.200a23]

The contaminants are ended by accomplishing the eightfold path.

17. The Factors of the Noble Path [T 99.200a28]

The noble path consists of eight factors.

18. Cultivation of the Noble Path [T 99.200b04]

The noble path is cultivated by relying on seclusion, having no desire, cessation, and an orientation towards renunciation.

19. Past and Future Cultivation [T 99.200b11]

A monk's past and future cultivation is by relying on seclusion, having no desire, cessation, and an orientation towards renunciation.

20. Arising of the Noble Path (SN 45.16: Purified (1st)) [T 99.200b15]

The pure, clean, and faultless accomplishment of the Noble Path only occurs when taught by a Buddha.

21. Arising of the Noble Path (2) (SN 45.17: Purified (2nd) ) [T 99.200b22]

The pure, clean, and faultless accomplishment of the Noble Path only occurs when taught by a Sugata.

22. Wholesome and Unwholesome Aggregates (AN 5.52, SN 47.5, and SN 47.45) [T 99.200b23]

The five hindrances are an unwholesome aggregate while the factors of the eightfold path are a wholesome aggregate.

23. Good Friends (SN 45.2: Half the Spiritual Life) [T 99.200c3]

Ānanda considers that good friends are half the spiritual path. The Buddha tells him that they are the whole of the spiritual path, for it’s why he teaches the noble path to sentient beings.

24. The Brahmin's Chariot (SN 45.4: Regarding the Brahmin Jāṇussoṇi) [T 99.200c11]

When he goes to the city for alms one morning, Ānanda encounters a brahmin and his followers leaving. The brahmin rode a white chariot with white horses, and everyone in the group was dressed in white. Onlookers hailed the brahmin’s chariot as the Brahma vehicle. When Ānanda reports this to the Buddha, he asks if the Buddha’s teaching has such a vehicle. The Buddha replies that it does: It’s called the eightfold path.

25. Wrong and Right (SN 45.21: The Wrong Way) [T 99.201a09]

The Buddha exhorts the monks to abandon the wrong path and cultivate the right eightfold path.

26. The Other Shore (AN 10.117 and SN 45.34) [T 99.201a25]

A brahmin asks the Buddha what is the other shore and what is not the other shore. He tells him that the right eightfold path is the other shore and the wrong path is not the other shore.

27. The Other Shore (2) (AN 10.117 and SN 45.34) [T 99.201b11]

A certain monk asks the Buddha what is the other shore and what is not the other shore. He tells him that the right eightfold path is the other shore and the wrong path is not the other shore.

28. The Other Shore (3) (AN 10.117 and SN 45.34) [T 99.201b11]

Ānanda asks the Buddha what is the other shore and what is not the other shore. He tells him that the right eightfold path is the other shore and the wrong path is not the other shore.

29. The Other Shore (4) (AN 10.117 and SN 45.34) [T 99.201b11]

The Buddha explains what is the other shore and what is not the other shore. The right eightfold path is the other shore, and the wrong path is not the other shore.

30. What’s Rightly Considered (SN 45.83 and 90) [T 99.201b13]

The Buddha explains that rightly consideration leads to the end of bad and unwholesome things, including the wrong eightfold path. When things are not rightly considered, then bad and unwholesome things arise like the wrong eightfold path.
