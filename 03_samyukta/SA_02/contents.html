The Related Discourses

1. The Sense Bases (cf. SN 35: Saḷāyatana saṃyutta)

This group of sutras has the common theme of describing the five aggregates, and how they relate to the Buddhist practice. These aggregates are form, feeling, perception, intention, and consciousness. The five aggregates represent the things that people can identify with as a self, and the constituents of everyday experience. When they come together, they form sentient beings, which in Buddhist thought includes animals, humans, and other beings like spirits, demons, and gods.

This group consists of 110 full sutras plus 68 abbreviated sutra variants for a total of 178 sutras. Below is a list of the English translations currently available with brief synopses of their contents. Pali equivalents are listed in () and the sourcetext is indicated in [].

Note: All titles for SA sutras are supplied by the translator as an aid for the reader.

1. Impermanent (SN 22.12, 22.51) [T 99.1a6]

When rightly contemplated, form is recognized to be impermanent, and so are feeling, perception, intention, and consciousness. That contemplation leads to disillusionment and dispassion, which lead to liberation.

2. Painful (SN 22.13, 22.51) [T 99.1a14]

When rightly contemplated, form is recognized to be painful, and so are feeling, perception, intention, and consciousness. That contemplation leads to disillusionment and dispassion, which lead to liberation.

3. Empty (SN 22.51) [T 99.1a14]

When rightly contemplated, form is recognized to be empty, and so are feeling, perception, intention, and consciousness. That contemplation leads to disillusionment and dispassion, which lead to liberation.

4. Not Self (SN 22.14, 22.51) [T 99.1a14]

When rightly contemplated, form is recognized to be not self, and so are feeling, perception, intention, and consciousness. That contemplation leads to disillusionment and dispassion, which lead to liberation.

5. Properly Considered (SN 22.52: End of Relishing (2)) [T 99.1a15]

6. Not Knowing (SN 22.24: Directly Knowing) [T 99.1a28]

Knowing and abandoning desire for the aggregates, one is able to end suffering.

7. Not Knowing (2) (SN 22.24: Directly Knowing) [T 99.1b6]

Knowing and abandoning desire for the aggregates, one’s mind can be liberated and transcend the fear of birth, old age, illness, and death.

8. Not Knowing (3) (SN 22.24 Directly Knowing) [T 99.1b16]

Knowing and abandoning desire for the aggregates, one’s mind can be liberated and end suffering.

9. Not Knowing (4) (SN 22.24 Directly Knowing) [T 99.1b16]

10. Delighting in Form (SN 22.29: Taking Pleasure) [T 99.1c11]

Delighting in aggregates is the same as delighting in suffering, which makes liberation from them impossible.
