  <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618a28'></td>
  <td class='subheading'>Who to Teach First</td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618a28'>爾時，世尊便作是念： 「我今以得此甚深之法，難解、難了、難曉、難知。 極微極妙智所覺知。 我今當先與誰說法？ 使解吾法者是誰？」</td>
    <td id='p2'>2. It was then that the Bhagavān had this thought, “Now, I have attained this profound Dharma that’s hard to understand, hard to comprehend, hard to realize, and hard to know. It’s an extremely subtle and sublime knowledge that I’ve realized and known. Now, to whom will I explain this teaching first? Who’ll understand this teaching of mine?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b2'>爾時，世尊便作是念： 「羅勒迦藍諸根純熟。 應先得度。 又且，待我有法。」</td>
    <td id='p3'>3. The Bhagavān then thought, “Āḷāra Kālāma’s faculties are fully matured. He should be the first to be liberated. He had provided me with the Dharma, too.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b3'>作此念已，虛空中有天白世尊曰： 「羅勒迦藍死已七日。」</td>
    <td id='p4'>4. After he had that thought, there was a god in the sky that said to the Bhagavān, “Āḷāra Kālāma died seven days ago.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b5'>是時，世尊復作念曰： 「何其苦哉！不聞吾法，而取命終。 設當聞吾法者，即得解脫。」</td>
    <td id='p5'>5. The Bhagavān again thought, “How terrible for him! He didn’t hear my Dharma before he reached the end of his life. If he had heard my Dharma, he would’ve been liberated immediately.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b7'>是時，世尊復作是念： 「我今先與誰說法？ 使得解脫？ 今，欝頭藍弗先應得度。 當與說之。 聞吾法已，先得解脫。」</td>
    <td id='p6'>6. The Bhagavān again had this thought, “To whom will I explain this Dharma first? Who could it liberate? Now, Udraka Rāmaputra should be the first to be liberated. I will give him a teaching. After he hears my Dharma, he’ll be the first to be liberated.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b9'>世尊作是念，虛空中有天語言： 「昨日夜半，以取命終。」</td>
    <td id='p7'>7. When the Bhagavān had that thought, a god in the sky told him, “Just yesterday night, he reached the end of his life.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b10'>是時，世尊便作是念： 「欝頭藍弗何其苦哉！不聞吾法，而取命過。 設得聞吾法者，即得解脫。」</td>
    <td id='p8'>8. The Bhagavān then thought, “How terrible for Udraka Rāmaputra! He didn’t to hear my Dharma before he reached the end of his life. If he had heard my Dharma, he would have been liberated immediately.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b13'>爾時，世尊復作是念： 「誰先聞法而得解脫？」 是時，世尊重更思惟：「五比丘多所饒益。 我初生時，追隨吾後。」</td>
    <td id='p9'>9. The Bhagavān then thought, “Who’ll be the first to hear this Dharma and attain liberation?” The Bhagavān then contemplated this once again: “There were five monks who were of great benefit to me. They had followed me since I was first born.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618b15'>是時，世尊復作是念： 「今五比丘竟為所在？」 即以天眼觀五比丘，乃在波羅㮈、仙人、鹿園所止之處。 「我今當往，先與五比丘說法。 聞吾法已，當得解脫。」</td>
    <td id='p10'>10. Again, the Bhagavān thought, “Where are those five monks now?” He then used his heavenly eye to observe the five monks, who were residing at the Residence of Sages in the deer preserve of Bārāṇasī. “I’ll go there now, and they’ll be the first to whom I explain this teaching. After they hear my teaching, they’ll be liberated.”</td>
  </tr>

</tr>

  </ul></td>

    <td id='p12'>12. After he spoke these verses, the Bhagavān then rose from his seat and set out for Bārāṇasī.</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c1'></td>
  <td class='subheading'>The Ascetic Upaka</td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c1'>是時，優毘伽梵志遙見世尊。 光色炳然，翳日月明。 見已，白世尊曰： 「瞿曇，師主今為所在？ 為依何人出家學道？ 恒喜說何法教？ 為從何來？為欲所至？」</td>
    <td id='p13'>13. It was then that the ascetic Upaka saw the Bhagavān from afar. His glowing form was clear and bright, blotting out the light of the sun and moon. Upon seeing him, Upaka said to the Bhagavān, “Gautama, where is your teacher? On whom did you rely when you left home to train on the path? About what Dharma teaching do you always delight discussing? Where are you coming from, and where are you going?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c4'>爾時，世尊向彼梵志，而說此偈：</td>
    <td id='p14'>14. The Bhagavān then spoke these verses to that ascetic:</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c6'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>「我成阿羅漢，<br/>
    世間最無比。<br/>
    天及世間人，<br/>
    我今最為上。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>“I’ve become an <em>arhat</em>,<br/>
    Supreme and without compare in the world.<br/>
    Among gods and worldly people,<br/>
    I am now the highest.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c8'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>我亦無師保，<br/>
    亦復無與等。<br/>
    獨尊無過者，<br/>
    冷而無復溫。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>“I have neither a teacher<br/>
    Nor any other equal, either.<br/>
    I’m the only worthy who’s without flaw,<br/>
    Who’s cooled and won’t be warmed again.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c10'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>今當轉法輪，<br/>
    往詣加尸邦。<br/>
    今以甘露藥，<br/>
    開彼盲冥者。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>I will now turn the Dharma wheel<br/>
    And go visit Kāśī city.<br/>
    Now, I’ll use the immortal medicine,<br/>
    To dispel their benightedness.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c12'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>波羅㮈國界，<br/>
    加尸國王土，<br/>
    五比丘住處，<br/>
    欲說微妙法。</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>The realm of Bārāṇasī,<br/>
    The royal land of Kāśī,<br/>
    That’s where the five monks are living,<br/>
    To whom I’m going to explain this subtle Dharma.</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c14'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>使彼早成道，<br/>
    及得漏盡通。<br/>
    以除惡法元，<br/>
    是故最為勝。」</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>Swiftly will they become awakened<br/>
    And realize their contaminants have ended.<br/>
    Eliminating the origin of what’s evil,<br/>
    Therefore, is the greatest thing.”</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c16'>時，彼梵志歎吒儼頭，叉手。 彈指含笑，引道而去。</td>
    <td id='p15'>15. That ascetic mockingly bowed his head and put his palms together. He then snapped his fingers with a smile, set out on the road, and departed.</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c17'></td>
  <td class='subheading'>Converting the Five Monks</td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c17'>時，世尊往詣波羅㮈。 是時，五比丘遙見世尊來。 見已各共論議： 「此是沙門瞿曇從遠而來。 情性錯亂，心不專精。 我等勿復共語，亦莫起迎，亦莫請坐。」</td>
    <td id='p16'>16. The Bhagavān went on to Bārāṇasī. Those five monks saw the Bhagavān coming from afar. When they did, they held a discussion about him:  “That’s the ascetic Gautama coming in the distance. He has a confused disposition, and his mind isn’t focused. We shouldn’t speak with him again, nor get up to look at him or ask him to sit.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c20'>爾時，五人便說此偈：</td>
    <td id='p17'>17. Those five monks then spoke this verse:</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td title='t125.2.618c22'><ul class='verse'>
    <li class='ch'>「此人不應敬，<br/>
    亦莫共親視。<br/>
    勿復稱『善來』；<br/>
    亦莫請使坐。」</li>
  </ul></td>
  <td><ul class='verse'>
    <li>“That man isn’t worthy of respect,<br/>
    Nor should he be befriended or looked at.<br/>
    Don’t again say, ‘Welcome!’ to him;<br/>
    Don’t ask him to sit down, either.”</li>
  </ul></td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c24'>爾時，五人說此偈已，皆共默然。 爾時，世尊至五比丘所，漸漸欲至。 時，五比丘漸起來迎。 或與敷座者；或與取水者。 爾時，世尊即前就坐，作是思惟： 「此是愚癡之人，竟不能全其本限。」</td>
    <td id='p18'>18. After the five monks spoke this verse, they fell silent. When the Bhagavān arrived there, he eventually approached the five monks. They then got up to watch him come. Some of them prepared a seat; others fetched water. The Bhagavān then took the seat that was readied in front of him, and he thought, “These foolish men aren’t capable of keeping their to own restrictions in the end.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.618c28'>爾時，五比丘稱世尊為「卿。」 是時，世尊告五比丘曰： 「汝等莫卿無上至真、等正覺。 所以然者？ 我今已成無上至真、等正覺，已獲甘露善。 自專念，聽吾法語。」</td>
    <td id='p19'>19. The five monks then called the Bhagavān “friend.” He told the five monks, “You are not friends to an unsurpassed <em>arhat</em> who is completely awakened. Why is that? I’m now an unsurpassed <em>arhat</em>, a completely awakened one, who has gained the virtue of immortality. Focus your thoughts and listen to my words of Dharma.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a2'>爾時，五比丘白世尊曰： 「瞿曇，本苦行時，尚不能得上人之法。 況復今日意情錯亂，言得道乎？」</td>
    <td id='p20'>20. The five monks then said to the Bhagavān, “Gautama, when you practiced asceticism before, you still weren’t able to attain the way of a superior man. How could someone whose mind was so confused say today that he has attained awakening?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a4'>世尊告曰：「云何，五人？汝等曾聞吾妄語乎？」</td>
    <td id='p21'>21. The Bhagavān asked them, “How is it, five monks? Have you ever heard me tell a lie?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a5'>五比丘曰：「不也，瞿曇。」</td>
    <td>The five monks said, “No, Gautama.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a6'>世尊告曰： 「如來、等正覺已得甘露。 汝等悉共專心，聽吾說法。」 是時，世尊便復作是念： 「我今堪任降此五人。」</td>
    <td id='p22'>22. The Bhagavān told them, “The Tathāgata, the Completely Awakened One, has attained the immortal. All of you, focus your minds and listen to my Dharma teaching.” The Bhagavān then thought, “I can train these five men now.”</td>
  </tr>
<tr>
  <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a8'></td>
  <td class='subheading'>Turning the Dharma Wheel</td>
</tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a8'>是時，世尊告五比丘： 「汝等當知，有此四諦。 云何為四？ 苦諦、苦習諦、苦盡諦、苦出要諦。</td>
    <td id='p23'>23. The Bhagavān then told the five monks, “You should know, there are these four truths. What are the four? The truth of suffering, truth of the formation of suffering, truth of the cessation of suffering, and the truth of the escape from suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a10'>「彼云何名為苦諦？ 所謂生苦、老苦、病苦、死苦、憂悲惱苦、愁憂苦痛，不可稱記。 怨憎會苦、恩愛別苦、所欲不得，亦復是苦。 取要言之，五盛陰苦。 是謂苦諦。</td>
    <td id='p24'>24. “What’s called the truth of suffering? It’s the suffering of birth, suffering of old age, suffering of illness, suffering of death, suffering of sorrow, and suffering of grief, which cannot be described. It’s also the suffering of association with what’s hated, suffering of separation from what’s loved, and the suffering of not getting what’s desired. Essentially speaking, it’s the suffering of the five proliferating aggregates. This is called the truth of suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a14'>「云何苦習諦？ 所謂受愛之分。 習之不惓，意常貪著。 是謂苦習諦。</td>
    <td id='p25'>25. “What’s the truth of the formation of suffering? It’s the factors of feeling and craving. When making a habit of them without tire, the mind is constantly covetous and attached to them. This is called the truth of the formation of suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a15'>「彼云何苦盡諦？ 能使彼愛滅盡無餘，亦不更生。 是謂苦盡諦。</td>
    <td id='p26'>26. “What’s the truth of the cessation of suffering? It’s possible to make that craving cease and end without remainder so that it doesn’t arise again. This is called the truth of suffering’s cessation.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a17'>「彼云何名為苦出要諦？ 所謂賢聖八品道，所謂等見、等治、等語、等業、等命、等方便、等念、等定。 是謂名為四諦之法。</td>
    <td id='p27'>27. “What is the truth of the escape from suffering? It’s the noble eightfold path, which is right view, right discipline, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right method, right mindfulness, and right <em>samādhi</em>. This is called the Dharma of four truths.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a19'>「然復，五比丘，此四諦之法，苦諦者眼生、智生、明生、覺生、光生、慧生，本未聞法。 復次，苦諦者，實、定，不虛不妄，終不有異。 世尊之所說，故名為苦諦。</td>
    <td id='p28'>28. “Moreover, five monks, in this Dharma of four truths, the truth of suffering is a principle never heard before that gives rise to vision, knowledge, insight, awakening, light, and wisdom. Furthermore, the truth of suffering is real, definite, neither fake nor false, and doesn’t ever change. It’s a teaching by the Bhagavān; therefore, it’s called the truth of suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a23'>「苦習諦者，本未聞法，眼生、智生、明生、覺生、光生、慧生。 復次，苦習諦者，實、定，不虛不妄，終不有異。 世尊之所說，故名為苦習諦。</td>
    <td id='p29'>29. “The truth of the formation of suffering is a principle never heard before that gives rise to vision, knowledge, insight, awakening, light, and wisdom. Furthermore, the truth of the formation of suffering is real, definite, neither fake nor false, and doesn’t ever change. It’s a teaching by the Bhagavān; therefore, it’s called the truth of the formation of suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a26'>「苦盡諦者，本未聞法，眼生、智生、明生、覺生、慧生、光生。 復次，苦盡諦者，實、定，不虛不妄，終不有異。 世尊之所說，故名為苦盡諦。</td>
    <td id='p30'>30. “The truth of the cessation of suffering is a principle never heard before that gives rise to vision, knowledge, insight, awakening, light, and wisdom. Furthermore, the truth of the cessation of suffering is real, definite, neither fake nor false, and doesn’t ever change. It’s a teaching by the Bhagavān; therefore, it’s called the truth of the cessation of suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619a29'>「苦出要諦者，本未聞法，眼生、智生、明生、覺生、光生、慧生。 復次，苦出要諦者，實、定，不虛不妄，終不有異。 世尊之所說，故名為苦出要諦。</td>
    <td id='p31'>31. “The truth of the escape from suffering is a principle never heard before that gives rise to vision, knowledge, insight, awakening, light, and wisdom. Furthermore, the truth of the escape from suffering is real, definite, and neither fake nor false, and it doesn’t ever change. It’s a teaching by the Bhagavān; therefore, it’s called the truth of the escape from suffering.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.619b3'>「五比丘，當知此四諦者，三轉十二行。 如實不知者，則不成無上正真、等正覺。 以我分別此四諦三轉十二行，如實知之、是故成無上至真、等正覺。」</td>
    <td id='p32'>32. “Five monks, you should know the three turnings and twelve steps of these four truths. Someone who doesn’t truly know them doesn’t achieve the unsurpassed, correct, and complete awakening. It was by discerning the three turnings and twelve steps of these four truths and truly knowing them that I achieved the unsurpassed, correct, and complete awakening.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>

He emerged from that <em>samādhi</em> of kindness and entered a <em>samādhi</em> of blazing light. The serpent’s fire and the Buddha’s light were produced right at the same time.</td>

Sometimes, he sat cross-legged and flew throughout the sky like a bird without any obstruction. He could also produce smoke without measure like a giant volcano.   <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c16'>是時，王告國中： 「諸有兄弟二人，當取一人作道。 其不爾者當重謫罰。」</td>
    <td id='p146'>146.  The King announced to the kingdom, “Wherever there are two brothers, one will be chosen to pursue the path. Those who fail to do so will be severely punished.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c18'>時，諸釋眾聞王教令： 「諸有兄弟二人，當取一人為道。 其不從教，當重謫罰。」</td>
    <td>The community of Śākyans heard the King’s instruction and ordered it: “Where there are two brothers, one will be chosen for the path. Those who don’t follow this instruction will be severely punished.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c19'>是時，提婆達兜釋種語阿難釋言： 「真淨王今日有教： 『諸有兄弟二人，當分一人作道。』 汝今出家學道。 我當在家修治家業。」</td>
    <td id='p147'>147. The Śākyan Devadatta said to the Śākyan Ānanda, “Today, King Śuddhodana has ordered, ‘Where there are two brothers, one will be chosen to become an ascetic.’ Now, you leave home to train on the path. I will remain at home and cultivate the occupation of the household.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c22'>是時，阿難釋歡喜踊躍。 報言： 「如兄來教。」</td>
    <td>The Śākyan Ānanda rejoiced and celebrated. He replied, “As your brother, I’ll heed the order!”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c23'>是時，難陀釋語阿那律釋言： 「真淨王有教： 『其有兄弟二人者，當分一人作道。 其不爾者當重謫罰。』 汝今出家，我當在家。」</td>
    <td id='p148'>148. The Śākyan Nanda then said to the Śākyan Aniruddha, “King Śuddhodana has instructed us, ‘Where there’s a second brother, one of them will be chosen to pursue the path. Those who fail to do so will be severely punished.’ Now, you will leave home, and I will live at home.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c26'>是時，阿那律釋聞此語已，歡喜踊躍，不能自勝。報曰：「如是，如兄來教。」</td>
    <td>When the Śākyan Aniruddha heard this, he rejoiced and celebrated, being unable to control himself. He replied, “Yes! As your brother, I’ll heed the order!”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.623c28'>是時，真淨王將斛淨、叔淨釋、甘露釋至世尊所。 爾時，駕四馬之車，白車、白蓋，白馬駕之。第二釋乘青車、青蓋，青馬駕之。第三釋乘黃車、黃蓋，黃馬駕之。第四釋乘赤車、赤蓋，赤馬駕之。 是時，諸釋有乘象者，有乘馬者。 皆悉來集。</td>
    <td id='p149'>149. King Śuddhodana then led the Śākyans Droṇodana, Śuklodana, and Amṛtodana to the Bhagavān. They rode in four-horse team chariots. [The first] chariot was white, had a white canopy, and was pulled by white horses. The second Śākyan rode a blue chariot with a blue canopy and was pulled by blue horses. The third Śākyan rode a yellow chariot with a yellow canopy and was pulled by yellow horses. The fourth Śākyan rode a red chariot with a red canopy and was pulled by red horses. There were also Śākyans riding elephants and others riding horses. They all went together as an assembly.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a4'>是時，世尊遙見真淨王將諸釋眾而來。 告諸比丘： 「汝等觀此釋眾。 并觀真淨王眾。 比丘，當知三十三天出園觀時，亦如此法。 無有異。」</td>
    <td id='p150'>150. The Bhagavān saw King Śuddhodana leading this assembly of Śākyans from afar. He told the monks, “All of you, look at that assembly of Śākyans. Look at that assembly of King Śuddhodana. Monks, you should know that when the Trayastriṃśa gods leave their pleasure garden, they do so in this way, too. There’s no difference at all.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a7'>是時，阿難乘大白象、白衣、白蓋。 見已，告諸比丘： 「汝等見此阿難釋乘白象、白衣不乎？」</td>
    <td id='p151'>151. Ānanda rode a white elephant, wore white clothes, and had a white parasol. Seeing him, the Bhagavān told the monks, “All of you, do you see that Śākyan Ānanda riding a white elephant and wearing white clothes?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a9'>諸比丘對曰： 「唯然，世尊。我等見之。」</td>
    <td>The monks replied, “We do, Bhagavān. We see him.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a10'>佛告比丘： 「此人當出家學道。 第一多聞，堪侍左右。 汝等見此阿那律不乎？」</td>
    <td id='p152'>152. The Buddha told the monks, “This man will leave home to train on the path. He’ll be the best in learning and worthy of waiting on me left and right. Do all of you see that Śākyan Aniruddha there?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a12'>諸比丘對曰： 「唯然，見之。」</td>
    <td>The monks replied, “Indeed, we see him.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a12'>佛告比丘： 「此人當出家學道。 天眼第一。」</td>
    <td id='p153'>153. The Buddha told the monks, “This man will leave home and train on the path. His heavenly vision will be the best.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a13'>是時，真淨王及兄弟四人，并難陀、阿難。 皆步進前，除去五好。 至世尊所，頭面禮足，在一面坐。</td>
    <td id='p154'>154. Nanda and Ānanda were in front of King Śuddhodana and his three brothers. They proceeded on foot after leaving behind the five fine items. When they reached the Bhagavān, they bowed their heads at his feet and sat to one side.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a15'>爾時，真淨王白佛言： 「昨日夜生此念： 『剎利之眾不應將梵志眾。 還將剎利眾。 此是其宜。』 我便告令國中： 『諸有兄弟二人者，便取一人，使出家學道。』 唯願，世尊，聽出家學道。」</td>
    <td id='p155'>155. King Śuddhodana said to the Buddha, “Last night, I had this thought occur to me, ‘The community of warriors shouldn’t lead the community of priests. They should return to leading the warrior community. This would be fitting for them.’ I then gave this order to the kingdom, ‘Wherever there’s a second brother, one man will be chosen who’ll leave home and train on the path.’ Please, Bhagavān, permit them to leave home and train on the path.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a19'>世尊告曰： 「善哉，大王！多所饒益。 天、人得安。 所以然者？ 此善知識良祐福田。 我亦緣善知識，得脫此生、老、病、死。」 是時，諸釋眾便得為道。</td>
    <td>The Bhagavān told him, “Good, Great King! That will be helpful. It’ll bring peace to gods and humans. Why is that? These good friends are an excellent blessing and a field of merit. Good friends are a cause for attaining liberation from birth, old age, illness, and death to me as well.” That assembly of Śākyans then attained the path.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a22'>是時，真淨王白世尊曰： 「唯願，世尊，教誨此新比丘。 當如教誨優陀耶。 所以然者？ 此優陀耶比丘極有神力！ 願優陀耶比丘恒在宮中教化，使眾生之類長夜獲安隱。 所以然者？ 此比丘極有神力。 我初見優陀耶比丘，便發歡喜之心。 我便作此念： 『弟子尚有神力，況彼如來而無此神力乎？』」</td>
    <td id='p156'>156. King Śuddhodana said to the Bhagavān, “Please, Bhagavān, instruct these new monks. They should be instructed like Udāyī. Why is that? That monk Udāyī has great miraculous abilities! May the monk Udāyī always be in the palace to educate us, making sentient beings gain well-being for a long time. Why is that? This monk has great miraculous abilities. When I first met the monk Udāyī, it made my heart rejoice. I then thought, ‘Even his disciples have miraculous abilities, so how could that Tathāgata not have such miraculous abilities?”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624a29'>世尊告曰： 「如是，大王。 如大王教，此優陀耶比丘極有神力、有大威德。」</td>
    <td>The Bhagavān told him, “Yes, Great King. As the Great King says, this monk Udāyī has amazing miraculous abilities and great majesty.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624b2'>爾時，世尊告諸比丘： 「第一弟子，博識、多知、國王所念所謂阿若拘鄰比丘是。 能勸化人民，優陀耶比丘是。 有速疾智所謂摩訶男比丘是。 恒喜飛行所謂須婆休比丘是。 空中往來所謂婆破比丘是。 多諸弟子所謂優毘迦葉比丘是。 意得觀空所謂江迦葉比丘是。 意得止觀所謂象迦葉比丘是。」</td>
    <td id='p157'>157. The Bhagavān then addressed the monks, “My first disciple to be widely known, recognized by many, and remembered by the King is the monk Ajñāta Kauṇḍinya. The one who’s able to encourage the people is this monk Udāyī. The one who has a quick wit is this monk Mahānāma. The one who always delights in flying is this monk Subāhu. The one who comes and goes from the sky is this monk Vāṣpa. The one who has many disciples is this monk Uruvilvā Kāśyapa. The one who can see the sky in his mind is this monk Nadī Kāśyapa. The one who can still his mind is this monk Gaya Kāśyapa.”</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624b9'>爾時，世尊廣與真淨王說微妙之法。 爾時，王聞法已，即從坐起，頭面禮足，便退而去。</td>
    <td id='p158'>158. The Bhagavān then broadly shared the subtle teaching with King Śuddhodana. After the King heard the teaching, he rose from his seat to bow his head at the Bhagavān’s feet and then withdrew and departed.</td>
  </tr>
  <tr>
    <td class='ch' title='t125.2.624b10'>爾時，諸比丘及真淨王聞佛所說歡喜，奉行。</td>
    <td id='p159'>159. Then, the monks and King Śuddhodana who heard what the Buddha taught rejoiced and approved.</td>
  </tr>
