The Medium Discourses

Chapter 5: Related to Cultivation

Sixteen short sutras make up the fifth chapter of the Medium Discourses, most of which have parallels in the Aṅguttara Nikāya. The common theme that runs through these texts is the presentation of causal chains that describe the progression of a monks advancement towards awakening.

English translations currently available with brief synopses of their contents are listed below. Pali equivalents are listed in () and the sourcetext is indicated in [].

42. What Purpose (AN 11.1: What’s the Purpose? ) [T 26.485a14]

The Buddha explains how observing the precepts ultimately leads to liberation.

43. Don’t Think (AN 11.2: Making a Wish) [T 26.485b20]

The Buddha explains that the progression from observing precepts to liberation takes place naturally that doesn’t require self-conscious review.

44. Mindfulness (AN 8.81 and AN 11.3) [T 26.485c22]

In this sutra, the Buddha begins the same progression from observing precepts to liberation by beginning with right mindfulness and right knowledge.

45. Conscience and Modesty (AN 7.65 and AN 11.3) [T 26.486a6]

The Buddha begins the progression to liberation in this sutra with conscience and modesty.

46. Conscience and Modesty (2) (AN 7.65 and AN 11.3) [T 26.486a22]

Śāriputra gives a teaching similar to the previous sutra by the Buddha, using a simile of a tree to make his point.

47. Precepts [T 26.486b23]

This sutra presents a simple and straightforward progression from observing precepts to liberation.

48. Precepts (2) [T 26.486b23]

Śāriputra gives a teaching similar to the previous, adding the simile of a tree with damaged or undamaged roots.

49. Veneration (AN 5.22: Disrespect (2)) [T 26.486c22]

This sutra presents a different progression that begins with having respect for others practicing the religious life and culminates in liberation.

50. Veneration (2) [T 26.487a16]

Similar to the previous sutra, the Buddha presents a progression beginning with respect for other practicing the religious life, but it then segues to a progression from guarding the faculties to liberation.

51. The Origin (AN 10.62: Craving) [T 26.487b4]

The Buddha explains that while the craving for existence doesn’t have a clear beginning in the past, it does have a clear chain of dependent origination that maintains it.
